Page 01 – Inlet & Shaft Area Fundamentals

Room Area: 4 × 5 = 20 m²

1) Inlet Area → 3% of room area

3% × 20 m² = 0.6 m²

2) Shaft Area → 3.5% of room area

3.5% × 20 m² = 0.7 m²

According to:

A₁V₁ = A₂V₂

The cross-sectional area does increase, causing velocity to decrease. We are trading off velocity for static pressure, allowing greater mass flow rate (total volume of fresh air delivered) rather than just local velocity at the entrance, as a suction effect is formed, pulling more air through the inlet.

Making internal shaft slightly larger than inlet, the necessary space is provided for the air to unfold and fill the duct without hitting the walls and creating rotational turbulence or secondary vortices.

Page 02 – Bell Mouth Dimensions

Ideal bell-mouth dimensions

Length-to-shaft ratio (L / d)

The axial length (L) of the bell-mouth should be: 0.55 × maximum diameter of exit shaft (d)

Exit shaft diameter (d): 94 cm

L = 0.55 × 94 = 51.7 cm ≈ 52 cm

B = 1.625 × 94 = 152.75 cm ≈ 153 cm

Entry corner radius (r / D)

The most critical factor for minimizing vena contracta (flow narrowing) is adding an entry radius (r) that is: 0.08 × entry diameter (D)

This specific addition jumpstarts efficiency by around 0.5% and reduces boundary layer thickness by 7 times, allowing the air to be closer to the aluminum and remove the heat away much faster.

The thickness provides the gradual turn air needs to stay laminar (smooth).

Page 04 – Discharge & Loss Coefficients

Relationship between Coefficient of Discharge (Cd) and Loss Coefficient (Ke)

Without bell mouth → Ke = 0.5

With bell mouth (first ratio set):

Cd ≥ 0.95 → Ke < 0.1

Formula relating Cd to pressure loss:

ΔPₜ / (0.5 ρ U²avg) = −2.207 Cd + 2.193

If Cd = 0.975 → Ke = 0.041

Notes:

  • To ensure air moves across the room, outlet-to-wall ratio should be 30%
  • Placing outlets at floor level provides more uniform distribution and thermal comfort
  • Cross-sectional shape: regular octagonal improves flow uniformity by 3×
  • Height should not exceed 3 storeys
Page 05 – Inlet Torus Gap

Integrate a nose cone at base or intersection of shaft:

  • Decrease stagnation
  • Expand flow area by 76.29%
  • Reduce energy loss (head loss) by 8.4%

Inlet Torus Gap

Cylindrical area of gap (where wind enters) must be greater than entry area of bell-mouth.

Area of bell-mouth entry:

πD² / 4

Area of 360° gap:

π × D × Hgap

Hgap ≥ D / 4

Range for Hgap: 0.25 D to 0.31 D → 38.85 cm to 45.9 cm Selected: 40 cm

Page 06 – Nose Cone Geometry

Why should the D gap be prioritized over 3% rule?

Using 3% rule gives gap = 12.5 cm Using Hgap ≥ D/4 gives gap = 40 cm

Using smaller gap would create a bottleneck, increasing Ke and energy loss.

Nose Cone

Shape: conical or elliptical profile with a sharper apex

As angle of apex decreases (becomes pointier), drag coefficient Cd decreases.

Optimal height: 0.5 r to 1.5 r

r = radius of shaft = 47 cm

1.5 × 47 = 70.5 cm ≈ 71 cm

Diameter of cone = shaft diameter = 94 cm

Nose cone profile sketch
Page 07 – Nose Cone Extension Logic

Purpose of nose cone is to eliminate stagnation point when air hits a blunt surface.

  • Nose cone acts as curved guide rail
  • Extending cone into shaft expands flow area by 76.29%
  • Prevents flow tipping and wake formation
  • Avoids massive turbulence at shaft center
Page 08 – Vanes Geometry & Logarithmic Spiral

Vanes thickness: 5 mm throughout

Vane parameters by depth:

  • 0–40 cm: Entry gap, vane angle 31°, width 27.5 → 31.3 cm
  • 40–71 cm: Transition, vane angle 31° → 0°, width 31.3 → 45 cm
  • 71 cm: Cone tip, vane angle 0°, width 45 cm
  • 71–112 cm: Guidance, vane angle 0°, width 47 cm
  • 112–122 cm: Mixing zone, 18° kick

Arc gap reduces from 40 cm → 26 cm → 24.6 cm

Logarithmic spiral from upper torus to bell-mouth entrance:

r = a e^(bθ)

a = 76.5 cm (starting radius)

b = cot(β) = cot(31°) ≈ 1.66

Using this ensures air always sees same vane slope and prevents stalling.

Page 09 – Vane Angle Progression

Unwinding the vanes from 31° → 0° is done through a parabolic progression instead of linear.

Formula:

β(x) = βinlet (1 − (x / L)²)

x = current depth (0 → 52 cm)

L = total axial length (52 cm)

βinlet = 31°

Page 10 – Micro-Scale Vortex Generators (MVG)

MVG (Micro-scale vortex generators)

  • 10%–50% of local boundary layer thickness
  • Taller → parasitic drag
  • Short → cannot draw momentum air towards wall

Airfoil profiling gives ~27.68% reduction in drag and stronger vortices downstream.

Spacing ratio:

λ / h = 5

Prevents flow separation, ensures air fills full 153 cm exit instead of leaving as narrow turbulent jet.

Placement ratio in conical diffuser:

x / L = 0.05 to 0.10

x = distance from start of diffuser L = total diffuser length

x = 153 × 0.05 = 7.65 cm x = 153 × 0.10 = 15.3 cm

→ Place MVG ~15 cm down diffuser
