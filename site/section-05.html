Micro-Scale Vortex Generators (MVGs)

Purpose

Micro-vortex generators were explored as a conceptual boundary-layer control strategy to mitigate flow separation under adverse pressure gradients in diffuser-type geometries. Their inclusion is based on established aerodynamic literature and is treated as exploratory rather than optimized for this specific passive ventilation system.

MVG Sizing (Height)

Literature-supported range:

h / δ ≈ 0.2 to 0.4

Where:

  • h = MVG height
  • δ = local boundary-layer thickness

Design choice for this study:

h ≈ 0.3δ

Rationale:

  • Heights below ~0.2δ generate weak vortices
  • Heights above ~0.5δ introduce excessive parasitic drag
  • A mid-range value balances vortex strength and drag, consistent with diffuser studies

Airfoil-shaped MVGs were considered based on literature reporting improved vortex coherence and reduced form drag relative to rectangular geometries. Reported drag reductions in controlled studies are noted as contextual references, not as predicted outcomes for this design.

MVG Length

l / h ≈ 4 to 6

This ratio supports:

  • Stable vortex formation
  • Delayed vortex breakdown downstream

MVG Spacing

Literature-supported range:

λ / h ≈ 8 to 15

Design choice for this study:

λ ≈ 10h

Rationale:

  • Prevents vortex interaction and cancellation
  • Maintains uniform boundary-layer energization
  • Avoids unnecessary drag from excessive MVG density

Placement Within the Conical Diffuser

MVGs are most effective when placed just upstream of the region where adverse pressure gradients begin to dominate, rather than at the diffuser entrance or exit.

Placement guideline from literature:

x / L ≈ 0.05 to 0.15

Where:

  • x = distance from start of diffuser
  • L = total diffuser length

For this geometry:

  • L = 153 cm
  • x = 7.5 to 23 cm

Design placement:

x ≈ 15 cm

This position corresponds to the early diffuser expansion zone, where boundary-layer separation risk begins to increase.

MVG Orientation and Offset

Yaw Angle

α ≈ 15° to 25°

Design choice:

α ≈ 15°

Lower yaw angles reduce drag penalties while maintaining vortex strength in low-speed flows.

Circumferential Arrangement

  • MVGs are offset from vane centerlines to avoid wake interaction
  • Circumferential spacing follows the selected λ ≈ 10h guideline
  • Placement avoids direct alignment with vane trailing edges

This configuration allows MVGs to interact with undisturbed, high-energy flow regions, maximizing boundary-layer re-energization potential.

Limitations

The effectiveness of MVGs depends strongly on:

  • Boundary-layer thickness
  • Reynolds number
  • Pressure-gradient distribution

Because these parameters vary in passive ventilation systems, MVG implementation in this project is conceptual and exploratory, intended to examine flow-control principles rather than to assert performance gains.
