Inlet & Bell Mouth Fundamentals

Inlet & Shaft Area Fundamentals

Room Specifications

Room Area: 4 × 5 = 20 m²

Area Calculations

1) Inlet Area → 3% of room area

3% × 20 m² = 0.6 m²

2) Shaft Area → 3.5% of room area

3.5% × 20 m² = 0.7 m²

Continuity Principle

According to: A₁V₁ = A₂V₂

The cross-sectional area does increase, causing velocity to decrease. We are trading off velocity for static pressure, allowing greater mass flow rate (total volume of fresh air delivered) rather than just local velocity at the entrance, as a suction effect is formed, pulling more air through the inlet.

Making internal shaft slightly larger than inlet, the necessary space is provided for the air to unfold and fill the duct without hitting the walls and creating rotational turbulence or secondary vortices.

Bell Mouth Dimensions

Ideal Bell-Mouth Dimensions

Length-to-Shaft Ratio (L / d)

The axial length (L) of the bell-mouth should be: 0.55 × diameter of exit shaft (d)

The entry diameter (D) should be: 1.625 × diameter of exit shaft (d)

[Visual: Bell Mouth Shape based on parameters d, L, and B]

Exit shaft diameter (d): 94 cm (from 0.7m² cross-sectional shaft area)

L = 0.55 × 94 = 51.7 cm ≈ 52 cm

Entry Diameter (D) = 1.625 × 94 = 152.75 cm ≈ 153 cm

Entry Corner Radius (r / D)

[Visual: Entry corner radius - r × d → 0.08 × 153 = 12.24cm]

The most critical factor for minimizing vena contracta (flow narrowing) is adding an entry radius (r) that is:

0.08 × entry diameter (D) = 0.08 x 153 = 12.24cm

Performance Benefits

  • This specific addition jumpstarts efficiency by around 0.5%
  • Reduces boundary layer thickness by 7 times
  • Allows the air to be closer to the aluminum and remove the heat away much faster
  • The thickness provides the gradual turn air needs to stay laminar (smooth)

Discharge & Loss Coefficients

Relationship between Coefficient of Discharge (Cd) and Loss Coefficient (Ke)

Without bell mouth → Ke = 0.5

With bell mouth (first ratio set):

Cd ≥ 0.95 → Ke < 0.1

Formula Relating Cd to Pressure Loss

ΔPt / (0.5 ρ U²avg) = −2.207 Cd + 2.193

If Cd = 0.975 → Ke = 0.041
